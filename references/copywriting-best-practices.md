# Google Ads Copywriting Best Practices

## Why This Matters

Google's algorithm rewards copy that matches searcher intent and landing page content. Better relevance = higher Quality Score = lower cost-per-click and higher ad position. The goal is copy that feels like the *exact right answer* to what someone typed.

---

## Headline Formula Bank

Use these as starting points — always adapt to the actual business, keywords, and landing page.

### Keyword-Rich Headlines (5–7 of the 15)

Pull from actual search terms. If someone searches "google ads agency hyderabad", they should see those words in the headline.

```
[Primary Keyword]                     → "Google Ads Agency India"
[Keyword] in [Location]               → "PPC Experts in Hyderabad"
Best [Keyword] Services               → "Best Google Ads Services"
[Keyword] Agency / Company / Expert   → "Certified Google Ads Partner"
[Keyword] That Gets Results           → "PPC Ads That Convert"
Trusted [Keyword] Specialists         → "Trusted Paid Ads Agency"
[Keyword] for [Niche]                 → "Google Ads for SaaS Brands"
```

### Benefit-Led Headlines (2–3 of the 15)

Lead with the outcome, not the service. What does the customer actually get?

```
More Leads, Less Wasted Spend
Ads That Actually Convert
Scale Revenue With Paid Ads
Lower CPC, Higher ROAS
[Outcome] Without [Pain Point]        → "Growth Without Guesswork"
Stop Burning Your Ad Budget
```

### CTA-Led Headlines (2–3 of the 15)

Start with an action verb. Be specific about the next step — generic CTAs ("Contact Us", "Learn More") don't click well.

```
Get a Free Google Ads Audit
Book a Strategy Call Today
Start Your Campaign This Week
Claim Your Free PPC Review
See Your Growth Potential
[Verb] + [Specific Offer]             → "Download the PPC Checklist"
```

### Social Proof Headlines (1–2 of the 15)

Numbers and specifics build instant trust. Vague claims don't work — "Trusted by Businesses" is ignored.

```
50+ Brands Trust Our Ads
₹10Cr+ in Ad Spend Managed
Certified Google Partners
4.9★ from 30+ Clients
[Number] + [Credibility Marker]
```

### Specificity / Urgency Headlines (1–2 of the 15)

Make it concrete. These work especially well in position 3.

```
Results in 30 Days or Less
For [Location] Businesses             → "For Hyderabad Brands"
No Long-Term Contracts
Free Audit — Limited Spots
[Season/Offer] — Act Now
```

## Description Frameworks

Google shows **any 2 of 4 descriptions** simultaneously — each one must make sense on its own and have a distinct angle.

### Framework 1: Benefit + Proof
Lead with the core benefit, back it up with a number or result.
> "Drive more qualified leads with data-backed Google Ads campaigns. 50+ brands trust us for PPC growth."

### Framework 2: Problem + Solution
Name the pain point the searcher is feeling, then position the business as the fix.
> "Tired of wasted ad spend? We build campaigns focused on ROI — not just clicks. Let's fix your PPC."

### Framework 3: Process + CTA
Briefly show what working with this business looks like, then a clear next step.
> "Free audit → custom strategy → managed campaigns. See what's holding your ads back. Book a call now."

### Framework 4: Offer or Urgency
Lead with any specific offer, guarantee, or time-sensitive element.
> "Get a free Google Ads audit valued at ₹15,000. No obligation — just clarity on where you're losing money."

**Rules for descriptions:**
- Don't start two descriptions with the same word
- End at least 2 with a strong CTA
- Include the primary keyword naturally in at least 2 descriptions
- Avoid stuffing — it should read like something a human would say

## Extension Strategy

### Sitelinks: Where to Point

Never send multiple sitelinks to the homepage — this wastes clicks and signals to Google you haven't thought this through.

**Map each sitelink to a different searcher intent:**

| Intent | Sitelink destination |
|---|---|
| "What do you do?" | Main services page |
| "Have you done this before?" | Case studies / results |
| "How much does it cost?" | Pricing or packages |
| "Who are you?" | About / team page |
| "I want to talk to someone" | Contact / book a call |
| "Tell me more about X" | A specific sub-service page |

**Sitelink title formula:** Action or section name
- "See Our Case Studies" (too long if over 25 chars — trim to "Our Case Studies")
- "Book a Free Call"
- "Our Google Ads Services"
- "View Pricing"

**Sitelink descriptions:** Reinforce the title's value in 2 short lines.
```
Title:  Book a Free Call
Desc 1: 30-min audit of your current ads
Desc 2: No obligation. Just actionable advice.
```

### Callouts: What Makes Them Hit

Callouts answer the question: *"Why choose this business over any competitor?"*

**Effective callout patterns:**
- Certification: "Google Partner Agency", "Meta-Certified Team"
- Guarantee: "No Lock-In Contracts", "Money-Back Guarantee"
- Speed: "Ads Live in 7 Days", "Same-Week Onboarding"
- Specificity: "₹50L+ in Ad Spend Managed", "50+ Active Clients"
- Differentiator: "Performance-Only Model", "Dedicated Account Manager"
- Coverage: "Google · Meta · LinkedIn Ads", "Full-Funnel Strategy"

**What to avoid:**
- "Quality Service" — every business claims this
- "Experienced Team" — meaningless without a number
- "Best in Class" — unverifiable and forgettable
- Anything over 25 characters

### Structured Snippets: Selection Logic

Pick the header that best reflects what the business offers — don't force a bad fit just because it exists.

**Agency / service business:**
Use `Service catalog` or `Types`
Values: the specific services — "Google Ads", "Meta Ads", "SEO", "Web Design", "Analytics"

**E-commerce:**
Use `Brands` or `Styles`
Values: top brands or product categories carried

**Education:**
Use `Courses` or `Degree programs`
Values: specific course or program names

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Character limits:**
Going even 1 character over means Google won't serve that combination. Count everything.

**Repetition across headlines:**
"Quality Ads", "High-Quality Campaigns", "Premium Ad Management" — these all say the same thing. Google penalises this. Each headline should cover a *distinct idea*.

**Generic callouts:**
"Professional Team" is noise. If you can't imagine a *specific competitor* NOT saying it, it's generic.

**Copy-landing page disconnect:**
If the ad says "Free trial" but the landing page asks for payment, Quality Score tanks and bounce rate spikes. Every promise in the ad must be fulfilled on the page.

**No CTA in descriptions:**
Searchers are in decision mode. They need to know what to do next. At least 2 of 4 descriptions should end with a clear, specific CTA.

**Sitelinks all pointing to homepage:**
This is the #1 sitelink mistake. It wastes impression share and clicks from users who want something specific.

## Ad Strength Quick Reference

| To improve from... | Do this |
|---|---|
| Poor → Average | Add more headlines (minimum 5), ensure keyword coverage |
| Average → Good | Add 3+ distinct themes, reduce repetition |
| Good → Excellent | Use all 15 slots, add 4 descriptions, cover all angle types |
